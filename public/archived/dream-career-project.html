My Dream Career Project Guide

Create an awesome 5-7 slide PowerPoint presentation about the career you want to have when you grow up. Show off your research and PowerPoint skills!

üöÄ Part 1: Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these steps in order to complete your project:

Step 1: Research Your Career (Find the Facts)

  • Pick Your Job: Choose one job you are genuinely interested in. (See the career ideas section below!)
  • Find the Education: What kind of schooling or training do you need? (e.g., college degree, specific certificate, trade school, on-the-job training).
  • Find the Skills: What personality traits and abilities are needed? (e.g., patient, good at math, creative, good communicator).
  • Find the Money: What is the average yearly salary? (You can use terms like "Starting Salary" or "Average Salary.")
  • Find the Future: Will this job be in demand in the next 10 years? Will it be easy or hard to find a job?

Step 2: Organize Your Slides (Plan the Flow)

  • Title Slide: Write your job title and your name.
  • Overview Slide: List the job description and 3 main responsibilities.
  • The Journey (Flow Chart Slide): Create your Flow Chart showing the steps.
  • Money & Future Slide: Put the salary and job outlook here.
  • Conclusion Slide: Summarize why this career is a great choice for you.

Step 3: Create Your Presentation (Design & Build)

Start with the Flow Chart (Mandatory!): Use the SmartArt or Shapes tool in PowerPoint to create a process graphic or flow chart showing the steps from "Now" to "Working in my Career." (Example steps: High School > College > Internship > First Job).
  • High-Quality Images: Find clear, professional images related to your career. Do not use blurry or cartoonish images.
  • Keep it Simple: Use bullet points, not long sentences or paragraphs. The text on your slides should be a guide for what you say, not a script.
  • Check the Rubric: Use the rubric to make sure you have included all 5 required elements.

üìù Part 2: Slide-by-Slide Breakdown

Your presentation should have 5 to 7 slides. Here is what should be included on each one:

Slide # Slide Title (Example) Required Content Visuals/Design Focus
1 My Dream Career: Marine Biologist Your Name and the Title of your Career. Use a clear, appropriate Design Theme.
2 What is a [Job Title]? 3 Key Responsibilities of the Job. 2-3 important personality traits/skills. A large, engaging professional image of someone doing that job.
3 The Path to My Career The Mandatory Flow Chart or Process Graphic (from now to the job). List the schooling/training steps needed. Clear use of SmartArt or connecting shapes to show the steps.
4 Money & Growth Average Yearly Salary (a specific number). Is the job demand Growing, Stable, or Declining? A simple Bar Chart or Icon to represent money or growth (optional).
5 Why This Job Is For Me 1-2 reasons why you chose this job and how it fits your interests. An image that represents you and your interests.
6-7 Bonus/Extra Slides (Optional) Use these slides to share a fun fact, a famous person in that career, or a quote. Use of an Animation or a unique visual element.

üí° Part 3: Dream Career Ideas for Research

If you're having trouble picking a job, here are some great ideas that are easy to research and have clear paths:

Creative & Design Careers

Video Game Designer: Someone who plans the world, characters, and rules of a game.
Architect: Someone who designs buildings, houses, and schools.
Chef/Baker: Someone who creates and manages a restaurant or bakery.

Science & Tech Careers

Veterinarian: A doctor for animals.
Computer Programmer/Coder: Someone who writes the instructions for apps and websites.
Astronaut/Aerospace Engineer: Someone who designs rockets or studies space.

Service & Community Careers

Firefighter: Someone who fights fires and provides emergency services.
Teacher/Professor: Someone who teaches students in a school or college.
Physical Therapist: Someone who helps people recover from injuries.

Business & Finance Careers

Sports Agent: Someone who manages athletes and negotiates their contracts.
Librarian: Someone who organizes information and helps people find books and resources.
Financial Analyst: Someone who gives advice on how to invest money.

Research Tips

  • Use reliable sources like Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS.gov)
  • Look at company websites and job postings
  • Consider interviewing someone in your chosen field
  • Check multiple sources to verify salary information

Design Tips

  • Choose a consistent color scheme and font
  • Use high-resolution images (avoid pixelated photos)
  • Keep text large enough to read from the back of the room
  • Practice your presentation timing
