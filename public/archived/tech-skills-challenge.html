Part 1: Microsoft Word ‚Äî The Research Report

Create a properly formatted research document

üîß A. Setup and Formatting 10 points

  1. Open Microsoft Word and start a new, blank document.
  2. Title: Type the name of your hero at the top.
  3. Font & Size: Select all text. Change the font to Calibri (or similar) and size to 12.
  4. Header: Go to Insert tab ‚Üí Header and choose a simple style. Type your name in the header.
  5. Save: Save your document as HeroReport_[Your Name].

üìö B. Content and Tools 30 points

Research and write a short, 3-paragraph report on your chosen local hero (3-5 sentences per paragraph).

Paragraph Focus Required Word Tool
Paragraph 1: Introduction Who is this person? What did they do? Where and when did they live/work? Use Bold on the person's name every time you type it.
Paragraph 2: Accomplishments What major challenges did they overcome? What is their most famous achievement? Use a Bullet List to briefly list 3 of their greatest achievements within this paragraph.
Paragraph 3: Legacy How did they change the community or the world? Why are they still remembered today? Use the Highlight tool to highlight one sentence that summarizes their most important impact.

‚úÖ C. Final Check

  1. Word Count Check: Your report should be about 150-200 words total.
  2. Print Preview: Look at the document using File ‚Üí Print (you do not need to print it) to ensure your Header looks right.

Part 2: Microsoft PowerPoint ‚Äî The Presentation

Transform your research into an engaging presentation

üé® A. Transfer Content & Theme 25 points

  1. Open PowerPoint and start a new presentation.
  2. Choose a Theme: Go to Design tab and choose a clean, professional theme that matches your hero's story (e.g., historical theme for an old figure, modern theme for a recent figure).
  3. Create 4 Slides: Title, Bio, Achievements, Conclusion.
  4. Copy-Paste Text: Use copy and paste tools to move content from your Word report into the slides, but do not paste full paragraphs. Summarize your points into short, easy-to-read bullets for the presentation slides.
Pro Tip: Remember that presentations should have bullet points, not full paragraphs. Keep text short and impactful!

‚ú® B. Visuals and Polish 25 points

  1. Slide 1 (Title): Add the hero's full name, your name, and a large, high-quality, professional image of the hero.
  2. Slide 3 (Achievements): Go to Insert tab ‚Üí SmartArt. Choose a graphic (like the Vertical Block List or a Hierarchy) to display the hero's 3 greatest achievements visually.
  3. Transitions: Go to Transitions tab and apply one simple transition (like "Fade") to all 4 slides.
  4. Design Check: Ensure all text is dark on a light background or light on a dark background so it's easy to read.
Image Quality Matters: Use high-resolution, professional images. Avoid pixelated or low-quality photos that make your presentation look unprofessional.
