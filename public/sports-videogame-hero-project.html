Part 1: Microsoft Word ‚Äî The Champion Profile

Create a professionally formatted athlete/gamer profile

üîß A. Setup and Formatting 10 points

  1. Open Microsoft Word and start a new, blank document.
  2. Title: Type your champion's name and their sport/game at the top (e.g., "LeBron James - Basketball Legend" or "Faker - League of Legends Champion").
  3. Font & Size: Select all text. Change the font to Calibri (or similar) and size to 12.
  4. Header: Go to Insert tab ‚Üí Header and choose a simple style. Type your name in the header.
  5. Save: Save your document as ChampionProfile_[Your Name].

üìö B. Content and Tools 30 points

Research and write a detailed, 5-paragraph profile of your chosen champion (3-5 sentences per paragraph).

Your profile should tell a compelling story about your hero's journey. Start with their background‚Äîwhere they came from, the challenges they faced, and how they broke into their sport or game. Include specific details like their hometown, early training, or first major competition. Make your reader understand why this person's story matters.

When describing their achievements, go beyond just listing awards. Explain what these accomplishments mean. For example, don't just say "Won 3 championships"‚Äîexplain the difficulty, the competition they faced, or records they broke. Use statistics to paint a picture of dominance: career averages, win percentages, tournament placements, or world rankings. Numbers tell the story of greatness!

Paragraph Focus Required Word Tool
Paragraph 1: Background & Rise Who are they? When/where did they start? What was their journey to becoming a champion? Use Italics on any team names, game titles, or sport names mentioned.
Paragraph 2: Achievements & Records What are their greatest accomplishments? Include specific statistics, awards, or records they've set. Create a Numbered List (1, 2, 3) of their top 3 achievements with specific stats/dates.
Paragraph 3: Impact & Legacy How did they change their sport/game? What is their lasting influence on fans, other players, or the industry? Use Underline on any statistical numbers or records mentioned (scores, years, rankings).
Paragraph 4: Challenges & Adversity What obstacles did they overcome? Include injuries, personal struggles, setbacks, or controversies they faced and how they persevered. Use Highlight Color (Home ‚Üí Text Highlight Color) on key challenges or turning points in their career.
Paragraph 5: Current Status & Future What are they doing now? Are they still competing, retired, coaching, or involved in the community? What's their future outlook? Insert a Table (Insert ‚Üí Table) with 2 columns showing "Current Activity" and "Description" for 2-3 activities.

‚úÖ C. Final Check

  1. Word Count Check: Your profile should be about 250-300 words total (5 paragraphs with 3-5 sentences each).
  2. Print Preview: Look at the document using File ‚Üí Print (you do not need to print it) to ensure your Header looks right and formatting is clean.

Part 2: Microsoft PowerPoint ‚Äî The Championship Showcase

Transform your research into an engaging sports/gaming presentation

üé® A. Transfer Content & Theme 15 points

  1. Open PowerPoint and start a new presentation.
  2. Choose a Theme: Go to Design tab and choose a dynamic, colorful theme that matches your champion's energy (avoid plain white themes).
  3. Create 7 Slides: Title, Background & Rise, Achievements, Impact & Legacy, Challenges Overcome, Current Status, and Closing/Thank You slide.
  4. Copy-Paste Text: Use copy and paste tools to move content from your Word profile into the slides, but convert paragraphs into short bullet points. Keep text minimal and focus on visuals!
Different Focus: While the local hero project emphasized text formatting, this project emphasizes visual Microsoft Office skills - charts, shapes, icons, animations, and effects!

‚ú® B. Visuals and Polish 35 points

  1. Slide 1 (Title): Add your champion's full name, their sport/game, your name, and insert a high-quality action photo. Use Insert tab ‚Üí Shapes to add a colored rectangle behind your title text to make it pop.
  2. Slide 2 (Background & Rise): Insert at least 2 images related to their early career or sport/game. Use Picture Tools ‚Üí Artistic Effects to apply a cool effect to one image. Add 3-4 bullet points about their origins.
  3. Slide 3 (Achievements & Records): Go to Insert tab ‚Üí Chart. Create a bar chart or column chart showing their top 3-4 statistics (like career points, championships won, tournament victories, win percentages). Make sure the chart has a title and labels.
  4. Slide 4 (Impact & Legacy): Add Insert tab ‚Üí Icons to include 3-4 relevant icons (trophy, star, gaming controller, sports equipment). Write 3-4 bullet points about their influence on the sport/game and fans.
  5. Slide 5 (Challenges Overcome): Use Insert tab ‚Üí SmartArt to create a visual representation of 2-3 major challenges they faced. Choose a timeline or process SmartArt graphic to show their journey through adversity.
  6. Slide 6 (Current Status & Future): Insert a professional photo of them today. Add Insert tab ‚Üí Text Box with interesting facts about what they're doing now (coaching, business ventures, media work, philanthropy). Use at least 2 text boxes with different formatting.
  7. Slide 7 (Closing/Thank You): Create an inspiring closing slide with a memorable quote from your champion (use quotation marks and cite the source). Add a "Thank You for Watching!" message and include one final impactful image.
  8. Animations: Go to Animations tab and add entrance animations (like "Fly In," "Zoom," or "Fade") to at least 5 elements across your slides. Make sure the animations enhance the presentation, not distract from it.
  9. Transitions: Go to Transitions tab and apply slide transitions between all slides. Use consistent transitions (choose one or two types) for a professional look.
Make it Visual: Sports and gaming presentations should be dynamic! Use charts for statistics, SmartArt for challenges, shapes for emphasis, artistic effects on photos, and icons to represent concepts visually. Remember: Less text, more visuals!
